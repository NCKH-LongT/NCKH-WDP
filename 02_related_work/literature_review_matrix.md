# Literature Review Matrix

| Paper | Domain | AI Model / Method | Dataset | Evaluation Metrics | Main Contribution | Limitation | Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Paper 01 - Bolton & Chapman (1986) | Horse racing betting / handicapping | Multinomial Logit Model | 200 horse races | Prediction accuracy, betting return | Estimates win probability for each horse using race-level choice modeling | Traditional statistical model; limited ability to capture non-linear patterns | High |
| Paper 02 - Chapman (2008) | Hong Kong horse racing betting | 20-variable Multinomial Logit Model | 2,000 Hong Kong races | Holdout prediction, positive betting return | Extends Bolton & Chapman using richer variables and larger dataset | Still relies on hand-crafted variables and logit assumption | High |
| Paper 03 - Benter (1994) | Computerized horse racing handicapping and wagering | Logit-based handicapping + public odds combination | Horse racing data used in wagering systems | Improvement measure, betting feasibility | Describes practical components of a computerized horse racing prediction and wagering system | Focus is wagering system, not modern ML or tournament management | High |
| Paper 04 - Pudaruth et al. (2013) | Champ De Mars horse racing | Weighted probabilistic approach | 240 races from 2010 season | Winner prediction accuracy | Uses weighted factors to predict winners and reports 58% accuracy | Manual weights; limited generalization | High |
| Paper 05 - Davoodi & Khanteymoori (2010) | Horse racing prediction | Artificial Neural Networks: BP, Momentum BP, Quasi-Newton, Levenberg-Marquardt | AQUEDUCT race track data; 100 races mentioned in related summaries | Prediction accuracy, training time | Applies several ANN algorithms to predict horse racing outcomes / finishing time | Small dataset; neural networks less explainable | High |
| Paper 06 - Williams & Li (2008) | Jamaica horse racing | Supervised neural network algorithms | 143 races in Jamaica | Accuracy | Compares neural network algorithms and reports acceptable prediction performance | Limited sample size and location-specific data | High |
| Paper 07 - Gulum (2018) | Horse racing prediction | Graph-based features + ANN + Logistic Regression | Horse races collected from 2015 to 2017 | Prediction performance comparison | Shows that relationships among horses/races can be represented as graph features | Master thesis; not a deployed system | High |
| Paper 08 - Zhang (2022) | Horse racing competition decision management | Association Rules + Neural Network; B/S system structure | Horse racing competition data | Prediction accuracy / decision model performance | Combines data mining and neural network for horse racing decision management | Model details and dataset may be difficult to reproduce | High |
| Paper 09 - Colle (2022) | AI application in horse racing | Conceptual framework: computer vision, statistical learning, game theory | Literature and domain analysis | Qualitative review | Discusses future AI directions for horse racing and betting markets | Conceptual paper; does not provide experimental dataset | Medium |
| Paper 10 - Gupta & Singh (2023) | Horse race result prediction | KNN, Linear Regression, Random Forest, Gaussian Naive Bayes, AdaBoost, Bagging, feature selection | Horse racing dataset with thousands of rows and multiple attributes | Accuracy / model comparison | Compares ML algorithms with feature selection for horse race result prediction | Dataset context may be limited; model interpretability varies | High |

## Research Gap

Most previous studies focus on predicting race outcomes or improving betting strategies as standalone research problems. However, few works integrate win probability prediction into a complete horse racing tournament management system with user roles, horse registration, jockey assignment, referee confirmation, wallet transactions, virtual betting, ranking, and dashboard analytics. Therefore, our project can contribute by applying machine learning prediction inside a practical full-stack horse racing management platform.
