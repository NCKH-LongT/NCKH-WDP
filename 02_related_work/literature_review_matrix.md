## Research Gap Summary

Based on the literature reviewed above, the following gaps are identified:

| Gap | Observed in papers | Our proposed solution |
|---|---|---|
| No unified management + prediction platform | Papers 10, 11 manage horses but lack prediction; Papers 06, 08, 09, 12 predict but lack full management | Our system combines full tournament management lifecycle with AI prediction in one platform |
| Jockey-horse interaction rarely modelled | Papers 05, 09 note this as a limitation or promising direction | We explicitly include jockey-horse combination features in our XGBoost feature set |
| Cold-start recommendation problem rarely addressed | No reviewed paper proposes a hybrid recommendation for new users or new races | Our hybrid CF + CBF recommendation mitigates cold-start for new spectators and new races |
| Race simulation with AI not explored | Papers use prediction for ranking but not for simulating realistic finish order and time | Our LightGBM-based simulation engine generates realistic finish times with controlled noise |
| Spectator personalization largely absent | Paper 07 studies motivation but no system uses it to personalize race suggestions | Our recommendation module uses betting history and followed entities for personalized race suggestions |
| Virtual wallet and payment rarely integrated | No reviewed paper integrates virtual wallet or payment sandbox with prediction | Our system integrates Stripe sandbox, virtual coins, and automated bet settlement |
| Real-time race tracking not combined with AI | Paper 12 shows real-time web service but no live simulation | Our system broadcasts live race updates via Socket.IO alongside AI-driven position updates |
